Introduction

Ensuring the right talent is not just about recruitment — it also requires performance management and retention. The “Theory of Work Role Transition” suggests that an individual’s performance and retention depend on both personal and organizational adjustments, considering Role Requirements, Motivational Orientations, Prior Occupational Socialization and Organizational Adjustment. OLeD offers a comprehensive learning program based on these principles, designed to guide and nurture freshers.

Objectives

  • Understand the corporate environment and integrate freshers effectively
  • Develop personal & professional vision, aligned with organizational goals
  • Identify strengths and areas for improvement — personal & professional
  • Cultivate teamwork, leadership and collaboration competencies
  • Recognize and address psychological factors that influence effectiveness
  • Enhance collegial sensitivity and interpersonal relationships
  • Foster continuous personal and professional development

Methodology

This “Action Learning” program encourages participants to take responsibility for their own learning through:

  • Experimentation & Exploration
  • Discussions & Sharing
  • Reflection & Self-Discovery
  • Conceptualization & Theoretical Understanding

Total Duration: 24 days (customizable as per organizational requirements). The process-driven approach ensures deep learning and long-term development.
